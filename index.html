Fundamental Color Concepts

The Color Wheel

Primary: Red, Yellow, Blue  •  Secondary: Orange, Green, Purple  •  Tertiary: mixes of primary + secondary

Color Temperature

COOL WARM

Cool (blues/greens/purples) often recede and feel calm or professional. Warm (reds/oranges/yellows) advance and add energy or comfort.

Color Relationships (Diagrams)

Complementary

Two hues opposite each other on the wheel. High tension/contrast — use one as dominant, the other as an accent.

Split‑Complementary

A base hue plus the two neighbors of its complement. Keeps contrast while softening the clash.

Analogous

Three adjacent hues. Low tension, very cohesive backgrounds or UI shells.

Triad

Three hues spaced 120°. Vibrant and balanced if one hue is prioritized.

Tetradic — Rectangle

Two complementary pairs with unequal spacing (60°/120°). Extremely rich; pick a dominant pair.

Tetradic — Square

Four hues at 90° intervals. Balanced but busy — manage with value/saturation hierarchy.

Quick Reference Tips

For Dramatic Impact

  • Lean on complementary pairs
  • Push value contrast
  • Use bold, saturated accents
  • Balance with neutral space

For Harmony & Peace

  • Analogous schemes
  • Lower contrast / softer tones
  • Consistent temperature
  • Generous white/negative space

For Energy & Excitement

  • Warm‑dominant palette
  • High saturation bursts
  • Triadic compositions
  • Animated highlights (in UI)

For a Professional Look

  • Cool‑dominant or muted palettes
  • Monochromatic with accents
  • Subtle contrast & clear hierarchy
  • Test accessibility early
